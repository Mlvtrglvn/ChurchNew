Announcements

Last update: 19-9-2015

Church Feast
Our yearly St. Vincent’s church feast will be held on Sunday, September 27th (next week), after 10:30 Mass downstairs in the church hall. It will be combined with a farewell for Fr. Pasini and welcome to our new priests, Fr. Benedict Lam and Fr. Fabio Favata.

Advance tickets are $20 and may be purchased after Mass; tickets purchased on the day of the feast will be $40. Please bring a pan of food to share; pans are available outside the church along with tickets. Coffee, tea and juice will be provided. Funds from ticket sales will help cover the costs of the feast and any surplus monies will be donated to charity.
Raffle Tickets for Charity
From next Sunday, the Feast of Saint Vincent, who is the patron of charity, we will raise funds for Caritas, which provides various services to the Hong Kong community (including education, social work, and medical services). Raffle tickets will be sold after Mass for $20 each on a first come first serve basis. The drawing will take place on 8 November at Caritas Kowloon Bazaar.
Sunday School
Catechesis for children and young people will begin in October. Pre-Communion, post-Communion (as preparation for the renewal of the Profession of Faith), pre-Confirmation, and post-Confirmation (on Catholic ethics and moral values) classes are being organized. Please register outside the church.
Coffee Morning
Coffee morning after 10:30 Mass each week, and on the first Sunday of the month after 8:30 Mass, provide a chance for parishioners to get to know each other and share fellowship. If you enjoy these occasions, please take a turn to provide and serve snacks and drinks. Sign up sheets are on the bulletin board. Thank you!
Holympics
TOn Monday, September 28th (a public holiday), our parish’s delegation to the Holympics will meet at 8:30 am at St. Vincent’s to go to Sing Yin Secondary School 聖言中學 in Choi Hung for the event. Mass will be celebrated there at 9:45 am by Bishop Joseph Ha, followed by various sports activities and lunch. Parish youth and university students are particularly welcomed to join, even at the last minute. Tickets are $40, and you can register online here.
World Youth Day
With the consent of our bishop, St. Andrew’s and Tai Po parishes will together organize one of the diocesan groups to participate in World Youth Day in Krakow (Poland) in July 2016. Only 20 young people (16-35 years old) from each parish are permitted and will include both English- and Chinese-speaking youth. The deadline for registration is October 18th (Mission Sunday); enrollment forms and detailed information are available in the sacristy. Father Fabio and Father George will be in charge of our parish group.
